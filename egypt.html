Magnificent Sceneries of Egypt

Why Egypt is Special to Me

Egypt holds a magical allure for me as the cradle of one of the world's greatest ancient civilizations. Standing before the pyramids, walking through temple ruins, and cruising the Nile feels like stepping back in time. The scale of Egypt's monuments and the sophistication of its ancient culture never fail to amaze me.

Beyond the ancient wonders, I'm drawn to Egypt's vibrant modern culture - the bustling bazaars, the warm hospitality of its people, and the beautiful contrast between the desert and the lush Nile valley. The country's rich history spanning pharaohs, Greeks, Romans, and Islamic dynasties creates a fascinating cultural tapestry.

How to Get There from the Philippines

Traveling from the Philippines to Egypt requires some planning but is an unforgettable journey:

  • By Air: No direct flights. Common routes include Manila to Cairo via Middle Eastern hubs (Dubai, Doha) with total travel time around 16-20 hours.
  • Best Time to Visit: October to April when temperatures are milder. Avoid peak summer (June-August) when desert heat can be extreme.
  • Visa Requirements: Philippine passport holders can obtain a visa on arrival in Egypt (check current regulations before travel).
  • Special Note: Consider Nile cruises between Luxor and Aswan for a unique way to experience Egypt's ancient sites.

Other Must-Visit Places in Egypt

Luxor

"World's greatest open-air museum" with Karnak Temple and Valley of the Kings.

Aswan

Beautiful Nile views, Philae Temple, and gateway to Abu Simbel.

Alexandria

Mediterranean charm with Greco-Roman history and the modern Bibliotheca.

Red Sea Coast

World-class diving in Hurghada or Sharm El Sheikh's coral reefs.
